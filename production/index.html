Dependent on memory

You have to remember that Mike knows Dave. That Sara is connected to the VC you need. That Tom's cousin runs a title company in Austin. Nobody's brain can hold a 142-person network reliably.

Growth

How we counter problems related to growth.

Yes. Here are the real options, ranked by feasibility and trust preservation:

Tier 1
High trust, buildable now
LinkedIn OAuth import
User connects LinkedIn, you pull their 1st-degree connections with names, titles, companies. Map against existing DealNexus users. No cold outreach — just graph enrichment. LinkedIn allows this via their API (with restrictions). The catch: LinkedIn throttles heavily and may revoke access if you're seen as extracting graph data. Workaround: position it as "find your LinkedIn connections already on DealNexus" — same framing as Facebook's "find friends" feature. Legally safe, politically careful.

Email contact import (Gmail / Outlook OAuth)
User grants read-only Gmail/Outlook access. You parse the To/From/CC fields of sent mail — not content, just who they email regularly. Frequency of contact = trust signal. Someone you email 3x/week is a stronger trust node than someone you emailed once in 2019. This is how Superhuman, Mixmax, and Clearbit Reveal work. High signal, low friction, strong legal precedent.

Calendar import (Google Calendar / Outlook)
Who have you met with in the last 12 months? Meeting frequency = real-world relationship strength. A lender you've had 8 calls with is a much warmer node than a LinkedIn connection you've never spoken to. Calendar is arguably the highest-trust signal available because it reflects actual time invested.
Tier 2
Useful but noisier
Twitter/X follow graph
Pull who a user follows and who follows them back (mutual follows = weak trust signal). Useful for the startup/angel vertical specifically — many founders and angels are active on X and their follow graph maps loosely to their professional network. Not useful for RE lenders who aren't on X.

WhatsApp group membership (indirect)
You can't read WhatsApp programmatically, but you can let users manually add their WhatsApp group members by sharing a DealNexus invite link into the group. One share → everyone in that group gets an invite with context. This is organic but powerful — WhatsApp groups are where real estate deals already happen.

AngelList / Crunchbase profile link
For the startup vertical: user pastes their AngelList URL, you scrape their portfolio companies, co-investors, and advisors. Maps the venture graph without API access. Crude but effective for seeding the angel side of the network.
Tier 3
Interesting but complex
CRM sync (HubSpot / Salesforce)
Lenders and serious flippers already have their contacts in a CRM. A one-click HubSpot OAuth import would pull every contact they've ever worked with into the trust graph — pre-tagged by deal history if the CRM data is structured. High value, but only relevant for power users ($1K Partner tier). Could be a feature exclusive to that tier.

SMS/iMessage history (iOS Shortcuts / Android)
Who you text regularly is a stronger trust signal than who you follow on LinkedIn. Apple doesn't expose this via API, but users can voluntarily export their SMS history via a Shortcut and upload it. Niche but worth offering as a power feature.
Architecture
The right architecture
Don't pick one. Layer them:
Phone contacts → highest trust weight (1.0)
Calendar meetings → high trust weight (0.85)
Email frequency → medium-high (0.7)
LinkedIn 1st-degree → medium (0.5)
Twitter mutual → low (0.25)
Every data source feeds the same trust graph with a different weight. The AI then routes intros along the highest-weighted path, not just the shortest.
This is the moat deepener — competitors can copy one import method. Nobody has built a multi-source trust weight graph that gets smarter with every connected data source.
Caution
One caution on LinkedIn specifically
LinkedIn has sued companies for scraping their graph (hiQ Labs case went all the way to the Supreme Court). Use their official API only — it's restricted but legal. Never scrape. The OAuth import path ("find your LinkedIn connections on DealNexus") is safe and standard practice.
