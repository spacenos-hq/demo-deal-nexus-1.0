Market Demand Proof: Why Warm-Intro Infrastructure Is a Priority Now

This brief compiles third-party evidence from market research, sales tooling benchmarks, and practitioner communities. The pattern is consistent: teams spend heavily on prospecting, still report outreach friction, and repeatedly seek warm access over cold outreach.

Executive hypothesis: buyers already pay for discovery tools, but they still lack a reliable system for turning network proximity into approved introductions. This is the execution gap DealNexus is designed to close.

Synthesis: Problem Landscape

Observed Problem Evidence Implication for DealNexus
Trust deficit in cold outreach Nielsen: recommendations from known people are most trusted. Warm intros should outperform cold outreach on response quality.
Teams already pay for top-of-funnel tools Forrester/LinkedIn TEI: significant ROI and budget allocation for Sales Navigator. Budget exists; opportunity is execution layer after discovery.
Prospecting remains operationally heavy Forrester interviews cite manual research and cross-tool switching before implementation. Automating intro routing can remove hidden workflow drag.
Practitioner pain is visible in public communities Reddit sales threads show recurring concerns: sounding fake, low response, outreach fatigue. Clear need for trust-first, context-rich intro workflow.
